## 9. Understanding RUCA Codes (Rural-Urban Commuting Area)

RUCA codes are the USDA's classification system for measuring how "rural" a ZIP code actually is, based on population density and daily commuting patterns (i.e., where people actually travel for work and services). They run from 1 to 10:

| RUCA Code | Classification | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| **1–3** | **Metro** | Urban cores and suburbs. Residents commute to large metro areas. These ZIPs have dense healthcare access. |
| **4–6** | **Micropolitan / Large Town** | Small cities (10,000–49,999 pop). Have some hospital infrastructure but may lack specialists. |
| **7–9** | **Small Town** | Towns under 10,000 with limited services. Residents commute to micropolitan or metro areas for many needs. |
| **10** | **Rural** | The most rural classification. Low population density, long commute distances, and often only one healthcare facility (if any) within a reasonable drive. |

**Why this matters for Mason Jar's clients:** A hospital in a RUCA 10 ZIP code is often the only option for miles. When that facility's marketing team (usually one person) needs to communicate to their community — whether for patient engagement, provider recruitment, or now advocacy against funding cuts — they're speaking to a geographically dispersed, hard-to-reach population with limited digital infrastructure. That's a fundamentally different marketing challenge than metro healthcare, and it's exactly the problem Mason Jar is built to solve.

## 11. Key Takeaways for the CRO

1. **Mason Jar's sweet spot is the solo rural healthcare marketer.** Every campaign should speak to that person — overwhelmed, under-resourced, but ambitious.

2. **Erin leads with empathy, not features.** Mirror that in campaign tone. This is not a "we do SEO and PPC" pitch — it's "we understand your world and we'll make you look good."

3. **The Midwest is home base.** Start campaigns in MN/WI/IA where Erin has existing client relationships and local credibility (Hudson Chamber member, community involvement).

4. **Healthcare + Environmental is the dual vertical.** Don't dilute — campaigns for rural healthcare should be healthcare-only in messaging, even though the agency serves environmental clients too.

5. **The 6-month transformation promise is campaign gold.** "In six months, you'll have a cohesive brand that stands out" — concrete, time-bound, aspirational.

6. **Referral and partnership channels may outperform cold outreach.** Erin's business is built on relationships and trust. Warm intros from existing healthcare clients or complementary consultancies (Barlow/McCarthy model) are likely the highest-converting channel.
