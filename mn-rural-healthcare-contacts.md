# Minnesota Rural Healthcare — At-Risk Facilities & Key Contacts

## Purpose
This is a prioritized contact list of rural Minnesota healthcare facilities most vulnerable to federal Medicaid/Medicare payment delays and cuts. Contacts are marketing/communications leaders or, where no dedicated marketing role exists, the senior leader that marketing would report to (CEO/Administrator). Facilities are organized by risk tier.

**Compiled:** March 31, 2026
**Sources:** CMS Hospital General data, HRSA HPSA designations, USDA RUCA codes (via Mimi Labs), LinkedIn, facility websites

## How to Use This List

1. **Tier 1 first.** These are independents with no system safety net. Reach out directly — they likely have budget authority and decision-making speed.
2. **Tier 3 (Welia) immediately.** Existing client = warmest intro. Offer advocacy support before they have to ask.
3. **Tier 2 system contacts** are for positioning Mason Jar as a coalition communications partner. The VPs (Kim Carlson Deiss at Essentia, Stephanie Wenisch-Jurgens at Sanford, Frank Lococo at CentraCare) control system-wide messaging strategy. Local contacts (Stacy Neubeck, Dan Smilloff, Danielle Page) are the people doing the work day-to-day.
4. **Tier 4** are the facilities most likely to have zero marketing support — the ultimate ICP for Mason Jar. Cold outreach with a "Community Impact Kit" offer would land well here.

## Notes on Contact Research

- **Email addresses** were not publicly available for most contacts. Standard healthcare email patterns tend to be `firstname.lastname@[system].org`. For independents, check the facility website contact page.
- **LinkedIn is the best channel** for initial professional outreach to these contacts. Most have active profiles.
- **Mahnomen Health Center** has a CEO (Dale Kruger) but no visible marketing or communications staff on LinkedIn — this is a 10-bed hospital in the state's highest-severity HPSA county. If any facility exemplifies the "one-person team wearing every hat" persona, it's this one.
- **Welia Health's marketing coordinator** (Taylor Anderson) describes herself as handling "Dynamic Marketing, Creative Storytelling, Internal Communications, Visual Content Creation, Public Relations" — a textbook description of Mason Jar's ICP buyer.
